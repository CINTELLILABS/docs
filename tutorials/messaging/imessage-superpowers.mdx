# Introduction

iMessage Superpowers extend your [iMessage agent](/tutorials/messaging/imessage) with interactive **app cards** — branded bubbles backed by a real Messages app extension that render natively inside the recipient's Messages app. Instead of a plain text reply, your agent can send a rich card for payments, bookings, polls, order status, and other in-conversation experiences.

Superpowers are configured per number. When enabled, the agent decides when a card is the right response and sends it as part of the conversation.

<Info>
  **Enterprise feature.** iMessage Superpowers are available on Enterprise plans with a provisioned iMessage number. Contact your Account Executive or [reach out to sales](https://www.bland.ai/book-a-demo) to enable them.
</Info>

## iMessage App Library

The App Library is where you register the apps your agent can send cards from. You can add apps from three sources:

- **Catalog.** Pick from a curated list of common Messages apps.
- **Bring your own (BYO).** Register a Messages app your team already owns.
- **Partner.** Use an app supplied by a Bland partner integration.

Once an app is registered, you can create one or more **card templates** for it. A template is a reusable card layout with `{{variable}}` placeholders. When the agent sends the card, it resolves the placeholders from conversation context, tool output, or a pathway variable.

Reusable templates keep card copy and layout consistent across conversations and let non-technical teammates edit them without touching agent prompts.

## Superpowers and Capabilities

**Capabilities** are toggles you enable per iMessage number. Each capability injects guidance into the agent about when a card fits the conversation, and lets the analyzer decide, on any given turn, whether to send a card instead of a plain reply.

Typical capabilities include things like collecting a payment, booking an appointment, running a poll, or sharing a location request. You can enable different capabilities on different numbers depending on what each line of business needs.

Because capabilities live on the number, the same [pathway](/tutorials/pathways) or [persona](/tutorials/personas) can behave differently across numbers — one number can send payment cards, another can run bookings — without forking the pathway.

## Updating a delivered card

Once a card has been delivered, your agent can update it in place. The existing bubble redraws with new content — no new bubble is posted. This is useful when the state of the thing the card represents changes, for example:

- An order card moves from "Preparing" to "Out for delivery" to "Delivered"
- A poll card updates its results as votes come in
- A booking card confirms after the customer taps through

Updates preserve the conversation flow and keep the thread readable.

## Location sharing

Superpowers include a location-sharing flow. Your agent can prompt the recipient to share their current location over iMessage, and the shared location comes back into the conversation for the agent to act on — for example, to find the nearest store, dispatch a driver, or personalize a recommendation.

## Setting it up

Superpowers build on top of an existing iMessage number.

1. Make sure your organization has an iMessage-capable number. See [iMessage](/tutorials/messaging/imessage) for setup.
2. Ask your Account Executive or FDE to enable Superpowers for your org.
3. Register the apps you want to use in the iMessage App Library (catalog, BYO, or partner).
4. Create card templates for each app, using `{{variable}}` placeholders where the agent should fill values in at send time.
5. Enable the capabilities you want on each iMessage number.
6. Send a test message from another device to confirm the branded card renders and any update or location flow behaves as expected.

<Tip>
  Superpowers are per-number. Enable different capabilities on different numbers when you want the same agent to behave differently by line of business.
</Tip>
